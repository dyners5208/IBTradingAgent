MoomooTrader Agent Reference Guide

Complete rules, thresholds, and logic governing every decision the agent makes.

Last updated: 2026-04-29

What the Agent Does

The agent is a fully autonomous options and equities trading system connected to a Moomoo brokerage account via the OpenAPI. It operates in a continuous loop, scanning markets for high-probability setups, placing trades, and actively managing open positions — all without manual intervention.

  • 1
    Scan & Score

    Fetches 260 days of daily OHLCV data for the entire market universe, scores every stock on money flow and momentum, and identifies the top 5 candidates per market.

  • 2
    Select Strategy

    Chooses an options strategy (or stock trade) for each candidate based on direction bias (bullish / bearish / neutral) and the implied volatility environment (high / low).

  • 3
    Size & Execute

    Allocates capital using a score-weighted budget, selects the best strike/expiry, and places limit orders via the Moomoo API. Anchor leg fills first; protection leg follows.

  • 4
    Monitor Every 30 Min

    Checks every open position against take-profit, cut-loss, theta-exit, and roll triggers. Executes closing or rolling orders automatically when rules are met.

  • 5
    Intra-Day Re-Scan

    Every 30 minutes, re-scores the day's top-5 candidates and retries placing trades for any slots that remain unfilled, but only if scores are still bullish.

  • 6
    Wheel Strategy

    Separately, runs Cash-Secured Puts and Covered Calls on a pre-approved watchlist (WHEEL_UNIVERSE) with its own scoring filter and budget pool.

Agent Loop

In run mode the agent cycles indefinitely. Each loop takes the following actions in order:

StepConditionAction
First scan Market open + past buffer + not yet scanned today Full universe scan → place trades → cache top-5 candidates
Re-scan First scan done + market still open + unfilled slots remain Re-score top-5 only → place trades for still-qualifying unfilled slots
No scan All slots filled, or market closed, or in open buffer Print reason; skip to monitor phase
Monitor At least one market open (or HK trading day) Check every open trade for TP / CL / theta / roll triggers
Sleep — Sleep up to 30 min; wake early if scan trigger approaching
End-of-day report Both markets closed; report not yet generated today Generate daily Excel report; sleep until next session open
Edge case: If the monitor run itself takes long enough to push past the open-buffer threshold (e.g. woke at 9:42, monitor finished at 9:47), the agent detects this and overrides sleep to 10 seconds so the scan fires immediately rather than waiting another 30 minutes.

Full Universe Scan

The full scan runs once per market per day. It is intentionally heavyweight — it fetches historical data for the entire market universe to find the objectively best-scoring stocks, not just a pre-defined watchlist.

  1. 1
    Build Universe

    Dynamically fetches the full tradable US or HK stock universe from moomoo (several hundred to thousands of tickers).

  2. 2
    Fetch 260 Days of OHLCV

    Downloads daily kline data for every stock in the universe. ~260 days is required for MA200 calculation.

  3. 3
    Score & Rank

    Computes composite score for each stock. Takes the top 15 by score as the candidate pool.

  4. 4
    Filter by Options Availability

    Checks which of the top 15 have listed options on moomoo. Keeps the top 5 with options (or top 5 overall if no options found).

  5. 5
    Fetch Options Chain

    For each of the top 5, fetches the options chain and computes avg ATM IV. Selects the target expiry (21–60 DTE preferred, minimum 14 DTE).

  6. 6
    Build & Execute Trade Plan

    Selects strategy, strikes, and contract count. Places limit orders. Caches the top-5 list for intra-day re-scans.

Scoring System

Every stock receives a composite score in the range [−1, +1]. Positive = institutional accumulation + bullish momentum. Negative = distribution + bearish momentum. The score drives strategy selection and position sizing.

Composite Score = Money Flow Score × 0.60 + Direction Score × 0.40

Strategy Selection

Strategy is determined by two factors: direction bias (from direction score) and volatility regime (from options IV or 20-day historical volatility).

High Vol
IV or HV > 30%
Low Vol
IV or HV ≤ 30%
Bullish
score > +0.25
Bull Put Spread

Sell OTM put (~0.30δ, 5–8% below price) + Buy further OTM put (~0.15δ, 10–15% below). Credit strategy — collect elevated premium. Profits if stock stays above short strike.

Bull Call Spread

Buy ATM call (~0.50δ) + Sell OTM call (~0.25δ, 5–8% above price). Debit strategy — low-cost directional play. Profits when stock moves up.

Bearish
score < −0.25
Bear Call Spread

Sell OTM call (~0.30δ, 5–8% above price) + Buy further OTM call (~0.15δ, 10–15% above). Credit strategy — profits if stock stays below short call strike.

Bear Put Spread

Buy ATM put (~0.50δ) + Sell OTM put (~0.25δ, 5–8% below price). Debit strategy — defined-risk bearish play. Profits when stock declines.

Neutral
−0.25 ≤ score ≤ +0.25
Iron Condor

Sell OTM call (~0.25δ) + Buy further OTM call (~0.10δ) + Sell OTM put (~0.25δ) + Buy further OTM put (~0.10δ). Profits from time decay and IV crush in a range-bound environment.

Long Straddle

Buy ATM call + Buy ATM put (~0.50δ each). Profits from a large move in either direction. Suitable when a catalyst is expected but direction is uncertain.

Intra-Day Re-Scan

After the first scan, the agent re-evaluates the same top-5 candidates every 30 minutes throughout the trading day. This is lightweight — only 5 stocks are re-scored, not the full universe.

Purpose: If an order was cancelled (unfilled in the timeout window), the re-scan gives it another chance with completely fresh pricing. It does not blindly retry — it first re-checks whether the bullish signal is still valid.
RuleDetail
Triggered whenFirst scan done today + market open + at least one candidate has no open trade
Stocks re-scoredOnly the cached top-5 from the day's first full scan
Score thresholdComposite score must still be ≥ +0.10 to retry
Stocks with open tradeAlways skipped — has_open_trade() guard prevents double-entry
PricingFresh options chain fetched at time of re-scan (new mid-price, new delta selection)
BudgetSame score-weighted allocation as the original scan
When it stopsAll 5 slots filled, or market closes, or all remaining candidates fall below threshold

Position Sizing — Score-Weighted Allocation

Positions are not sized equally. The highest-scoring stock in a market gets 3× the budget of the lowest-scoring one, with linear interpolation filling the ranks in between.

weight[i] = 1.0 + (STEEPNESS − 1.0) × (1.0 − i / (n − 1)) At STEEPNESS = 3.0, n = 5: Rank 1 (best): weight = 3.0 → 30.0% of budget Rank 2: weight = 2.5 → 25.0% of budget Rank 3: weight = 2.0 → 20.0% of budget Rank 4: weight = 1.5 → 15.0% of budget Rank 5 (worst): weight = 1.0 → 10.0% of budget
Single candidate: receives 100% of the budget pool (weight = 1.0, no normalisation needed). Two candidates: weights are [3.0, 1.0], giving a 75% / 25% split.

Multi-Leg Order Sequence

  1. 1
    Place Anchor Leg

    The short/sell leg (or buy leg for debit spreads) is placed first. Wait for fill before placing protection.

  2. 2
    Wait for Anchor Fill

    Poll every 30 seconds. Single-leg (CSP/CC): wait up to 20 minutes. Multi-leg spread: wait up to 10 minutes.

  3. 3
    Place Protection Leg

    Once anchor fills, immediately place the protection/hedge leg with fresh pricing from the current bid/ask.

  4. 4
    Timeout → Cancel

    If anchor does not fill within the timeout, the order is cancelled. The trade is not logged as open. The intra-day re-scan may retry it in the next 30-min cycle.

Why different timeouts? Single-leg trades (CSP, CC) have no hedge-risk — you are fully exposed while waiting regardless, so it is worth waiting longer. Multi-leg spreads have protection legs whose pricing goes stale over time; a long wait risks legging into the anchor at a very different volatility than the protection was priced.

Why require bullish score for CSPs? Selling a put on a bearish stock increases assignment risk: you end up owning shares of a stock in a downtrend. The score filter ensures you only sell puts on stocks with genuine bullish momentum, where assignment (if it happens) is buying something you'd actually want to hold.

Trade Monitor

The monitor runs every 30 minutes on all open trades. It queries live prices and order fill status from moomoo and evaluates each position against all exit and roll rules.

Fill Status Check (Every Cycle)

Fill stateAction
All orders cancelled / failedAuto-remove trade from open log. Stock is eligible for re-entry next scan.
No fill yet (pending)Log "PENDING FILL" — no action taken. Continue waiting.
Partially filled (some legs only)Evaluate only filled legs for TP/CL. Partial tag [P] shown in output.
Fully filledProceed to full P&L evaluation and exit decision.

P&L Source

The monitor prefers moomoo's authoritative unrealised P&L from position_list_query (same source as the moomoo app). This eliminates discrepancies from stale bid/ask when markets are closed. If all active legs are present in the position list, that P&L overrides the price-derived calculation.

Rolling Rules

When a credit position approaches expiry (DTE ≤ 14) without hitting TP or CL, the agent evaluates whether to roll it forward to the next expiry.

RuleDetail
Roll triggerDTE ≤ 14 days
Must roll beforeDTE = 7 days
Net credit requirementRoll must be executed for net credit, or at worst a debit ≤ 10% of original credit received
New expiryNext available expiry date after the current one
New strikesSame strategy re-applied at the new expiry using current price levels
ExecutionClose current position (buy-to-close short legs, sell-to-close long legs), then open new position
If roll not viableHold until next monitor cycle; close before DTE = 7
